Three mexican main course classics

Recipes • 8 min

1. Tacos al Pastor

Ingredients

500g of pork shoulder (sliced)
3 tablespoons of achiote
120ml orange juice
50ml pineapple juice
1 onion (chopped)
50g of pineapple (chopped)
Corn tortillas
Cilantro (to taste; for serving)
Lime (to taste; for serving)


Method

Mix in a bowl the achiote, orange and pineapple juices. Add the pork to the bowl and marinate for at least 1 hour. (Marinate overnight for the best result).

Using a frying pan, grill (or fry) the pork until cooked and crispy.

Heat up the corn tortillas in a pan or skillet. Once warm, place the pork, chopped onion and pineapple inside the tortilla.

Crown with cilantro and lime to taste.

Serve immediately.


2. Chilaquiles Rojos

Ingredients

6 corn tortillas (cut into triangles)
2 cups of red salsa (homemade or store-bought)
100g of shredded chicken
2 eggs (optional)
Sour cream (to taste; for serving)
Cheese (to taste; for serving)
Frying oil
Salt (to taste)


Method

Heat vegetable oil in a skillet or pan over medium heat. Fry the tortilla triangles for around three minutes or until golden and crispy.

Remove the tortillas and dry excess oil with the help of a paper towel.

Inside the same skillet, add the red salsa and heat it up. Slide the fried tortilla chips inside and gently toss to coat them in salsa. Let them cook for around two minutes or until slightly softened.

Serve immediately.


3. Enchiladas Verdes

Ingredients

Corn tortillas
150g of chicken (shredded, cooked)
2 cups of salsa verde
Sour cream (to taste)
Oaxaca cheese (shredded; to taste)
Cilantro (to taste; for serving)


Method

Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).

Heat the tortillas in a skillet or pan until they're slightly softened.

Fill the tortillas with shredded chicken and roll them up. PLace them inside a baking dish (seam side down).

Pour the green salsa over the tortillas and top with cream and cheese to taste.

Bake for about 20 minutes or until the cheese has melted.

Optionally, top with fresh cilantro and serve immediately.
