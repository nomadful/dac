How to make homemade pizza

Ingredients

500g of flour
10g of yeast
250ml of lukewarm water
2 teaspoons of salt
400g of tomato (chopped)
Salt
Pepper
200g mozzarella cheese
Olive oil
Basil


Method

We'll start by preparing the pizza dough. Place the flour into a large bowl and set it aside. In a different bowl, mix the yeast with 250ml of lukewarm water until it starts bubbling. Using your hands, make a hole in the centre of the flour, add the yeast and water mixture, and mix with your hands to form dough. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let it rest for 5 to 10 minutes.

Add two teaspoons of salt to the dough and move it to a slightly-floured surface. Knead it until it is smooth, then cover with a kitchen towel and let it rest for half an hour.

Preheat the oven to the highest temperature and prepare two baking trays.

Press the dough to make it thin and crop two circles about the size of a ruler (30 - 40cm) and leave the edges of each circle a little bit thicker. Place both circles on the baking trays.

Spread the chopped tomatoes over each dough circle, and season with salt, pepper and basil. Add the mozzarella cheese on top, and any toppings you wish to add to your pizzas.

Place both pizzas into the oven and let them bake for around ten minutes, or until the dough is crispy and the cheese has melted.

Top your pizza with more basil (optional) and serve immediately.
