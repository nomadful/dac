Finding ten-minute timespans throughout the week to meditate is crucial in order to achieve a mindful state and lessen stress and anxiety. Not being able to find these timespans may be an indicator that you should start prioritising meditation. The truth is there's no "right" time to meditate, but rather just meditate whenever you have time. Other key things to keep in mind are:

1. Meditation can't be forced
2. It's okay to take a break from meditation

If you don't feel like meditating, you can try other methods to relax, such as taking a walk or stretching out.


So... how to meditate

If you do happen to find any of those ten-minute timespans throughout your week, the meditation routine should be really simple:

Allow yourself to be calm. Remove any distractions such as devices, and use your phone only to set a timer.

Meditation doesn't have to be boring. Play some background sounds that help you relax. You can try any from our sound collection within the app.

Focus in your breath. The main goal is to focus on your breathing and listen to your body.

Reflect on something (optional). You can also centre your meditation routine around an intention, a goal or a moment that you want to reflect on.


Meditation isn't complicated, but if you can't get through the ten minutes without wandering, don't worry. Remember to take it one step at a time.
